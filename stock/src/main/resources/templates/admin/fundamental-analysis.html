EPS Trend & Quality

This module evaluates the growth and consistency of earnings per share (EPS) over time. It identifies "Turnaround" candidates and penalizes volatile or loss-making operations.

Growth % = ((Latest EPS - Oldest EPS) / |Oldest EPS|) * 100
Scoring Parameters:
  • Earnings Status (40%): Identifies structural shifts (e.g., Turnaround Recovery or Weakening Trend).
  • Growth Trend (30%): High scores for growth exceeding 20% annually.
  • Profit Stability (20%): Penalizes "sign-flipping" where a company oscillates between profit and loss.
  • Consistency (10%): Measures the percentage of positive earnings years.
Safety Cap: If a company has zero positive earnings years, its total module score is capped at 35.

PE-Based Valuation

Standard Price-to-Earnings analysis adjusted for the volatility of the DSE/CSE.

5 - 10 Attractive
15 - 25 Fair Value
> 40 Speculative
DSE Context: The "Value Trap"
An extremely low PE (< 5) in the Bangladesh market often indicates financial distress or impending restructuring. Our engine flags this as the Distress Zone.

Intrinsic Value (Graham)

Based on Benjamin Graham's formula for the defensive investor, adjusted for the local market.

Value (V) = EPS * (8.5 + 2g)
Where g = average annual growth rate
Safety Floor: For loss-making companies, the system uses Net Asset Value (NAV) as the intrinsic floor. For stagnant companies (negative growth), an 8.0x PE multiple is used as a conservative baseline.

Size and Liquidity Proxy

Uses Market Capitalization and Outstanding Shares to estimate the risk of price manipulation and institutional liquidity.

  • Large-Cap (> 1,000 Cr): Typically higher liquidity and lower manipulation risk.
  • Mid-Cap (200 - 1,000 Cr): Balanced profile.
  • Small-Cap (< 200 Cr): Susceptible to high volatility and liquidity traps.
